Purpose and Context

This project is a fundamental component of my Product Management Bootcamp at CareerFoundry.

It centers around the concept of "MakeYourMap," a fictional company catering to both B2B and B2C markets, akin to the popular Google Maps platform. The project provides a comprehensive and hands-on learning experience for me to hone product management skills by working on real-world scenarios and challenges.

Objective

To research, design, and build product improvement to create user-generated content in a mapping app named MakeYourMap as the new product manager who just joined the team a week ago.

Duration

3 weeks project for Introduction to Product Management Course

Tools/Skills/Methodologies

  • Prioritization Methods - Google Sheets
  • Survey and User Research - SurveyMonkey, Zoom/Google Meet
  • Wireframes - Figma
  • Presentation - Google Slides

    Project Takeaways

    Desigining Effective Research Questions

    The foundation for validating ideas lies in crafting well-designed research questions. These questions serve as the bedrock for gathering insights, shaping our understanding, and ultimately validating the feasibility and relevance of our concepts.

    Tailoring Prioritization Methods

    The choice of prioritization methods relies heavily on their inherent characteristics and the audience involved. Selecting the right approach ensures a more effective and targeted alignment of priorities, optimizing our focus on what matters most for our project's success.

    Leveraging Personas for Impactful Storytelling

    Personas are versatile tools, not confined to a single purpose. They serve as powerful storytelling elements, enabling us to weave narratives that resonate with various contexts and needs. Their adaptability makes them valuable assets that can be utilized across diverse scenarios as needed, fostering a deeper understanding of our users' behaviors and motivations.
